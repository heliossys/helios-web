## 1. Default Credentials Everywhere

It's surprising how often we find routers, switches, and IoT devices still using factory default passwords. Attackers know these defaults and actively scan for them.

**Fix:** Audit every device on your network. Change all default credentials immediately. Use a password manager to generate and store strong, unique passwords.

## 2. No Network Segmentation

When everything is on one flat network, a breach anywhere means exposure everywhere. An infected laptop shouldn't be able to reach your accounting server.

**Fix:** Implement VLANs to separate different types of traffic. Guest WiFi should never touch your internal network. Critical systems should be isolated.

## 3. Outdated Firmware and Software

Unpatched systems are low-hanging fruit for attackers. Known vulnerabilities are actively exploited, sometimes within hours of disclosure.

**Fix:** Establish a regular patching schedule. Subscribe to security advisories for your critical systems. Consider automated patch management where appropriate.

## 4. No Monitoring or Logging

If you're not watching your network, you won't know when something's wrong. Many breaches go undetected for months.

**Fix:** Implement centralized logging. Set up alerts for suspicious activity. Even basic monitoring is better than none.

## 5. Relying Solely on Perimeter Security

A firewall alone isn't enough. Modern attacks often bypass perimeter defenses through phishing or compromised credentials.

**Fix:** Adopt a defense-in-depth approach. Combine perimeter security with endpoint protection, user training, and internal monitoring.
